Environmental Pollution

The Greatest Enemy of Mankind

Pollution is defined as the the presence in or introduction into the environment of a substance(s) which has harmful or poisonous effects. Pollution can take the form of any substance (solid, liquid, or gas) or energy (such as radioactivity, heat, sound, or light). Pollutants, the components of pollution, can be either foreign substances/energies or naturally occurring contaminants. Although environmental pollution can be caused by natural events, the word pollution generally implies that the contaminants have an anthropogenic source.

Forms Of Pollutions

  • Water Pollution
  • Air Pollution
  • Soil Pollution
  • Noise Pollution
